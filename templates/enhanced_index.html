Understanding Protein Structure-Function Relationships

Secondary Structure Types

α-helix (H): Spiral structure stabilized by hydrogen bonds. Common in structural proteins and often contains binding sites.

β-strand (E): Extended structure forming sheets. Critical for enzyme active sites and protein interfaces.

Coil/Loop (C): Flexible regions often containing functional sites and regulatory elements.

Structure-Function Principles

Active Sites: Often located in flexible loops between structured elements.

Binding Domains: Structured regions (helices/sheets) provide stability and specificity.

Allosteric Sites: Regulatory regions that change conformation to affect function.

8-State DSSP Classification

Our enhanced model uses 8 states for precise structural annotation:

  • H: α-helix
  • G: 3₁₀-helix
  • I: π-helix
  • E: β-strand
  • B: β-bridge
  • T: Turn
  • S: Bend
  • C: Coil
